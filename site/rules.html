Welcome!

Here’s how each season is organized — step by step, just like a real sports championship.

🏁 Stage 1. Qualifiers

  • Create a public YouTube playlist named “AI Games Challenge — Season 1”.
  • Paste your verification token in the playlist description (you’ll get it after registration).
  • Upload videos where you explain rules of your hybrid sport and show real gameplay.
  • Your leaderboard position is based on YouTube stats from your playlist (views, likes, engagement).
  • The most active and popular playlists advance to the Quarterfinals.

🎯 Stage 2. Quarterfinals

  • Teams that made it through keep developing their season playlists.
  • A new challenge is announced on the official AI Games Challenge YouTube channel.
  • You may publish one or several videos — it’s about keeping your channel alive and engaging.
  • Progress here is determined only by YouTube metrics. Top teams advance to the Semifinals.

🚀 Stage 3. Semifinals

  • Another challenge is revealed on the official channel.
  • Playlists keep growing with fresh videos and community activity.
  • Results are a mix of: YouTube stats (views, likes, engagement) plus a jury score for creativity, clarity, and team effort.

🏆 Stage 4. Finals

  • The ultimate challenge of the season is announced on the official channel.
  • Finalists showcase everything they’ve built: their game, their community, their creativity.
  • The winner is chosen by a combination of final YouTube ranking and the jury’s decision.

Notes

  • Eligibility: anyone with a public YouTube channel (solo or team).
  • Safety & fair play: no dangerous stunts; respect people, property, and YouTube Guidelines.
  • Anti-gaming: artificial boosting (bots, fake views) may lead to disqualification.
