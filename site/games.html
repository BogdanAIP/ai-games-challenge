Vortex (AI-generated, 2025)

Hybrid of football, handball and basketball. Three field zones with distinct control rules.

1. Field & Equipment

  • Indoor court: 40×20 m. Goals: 3×2 m.
  • Ball: size 4–5 (futsal or handball quality).
  • Zones (from own goal to opponent’s): Defense → Turbo Center → Attack. Lines are part of the forward zone.
  • Teams: 6 vs 6 + goalkeepers; rolling substitutions.

2. Core Zone Rules

  • Defense zone: control the ball with hands only (passing like in handball). No kicks.
  • Turbo Center: any control (hands/feet/body) but max 3 seconds per continuous possession; then pass/dribble or move the ball onward.
  • Attack zone: control the ball without hands/forearms (feet/body allowed). Goals count only if kicked from the Attack zone.

3. Scoring

  • Regular goal (foot shot from Attack) = 1 point.
  • Optional (pilot): “Golden pass” — direct completed pass from Defense to Attack that leads to a shot in ≤3 touches = +1 bonus (max 1 per team per period).

4. Restarts

  • Kick-off: from Turbo Center line, by feet.
  • Throw-in (out of bounds): by hands everywhere except in opponent’s Attack zone, where it is by feet.
  • Corner: by feet. Free kicks: by feet in Attack; by choice (hands pass or feet) in Defense/Center.
  • Goalkeeper restarts by hands in Defense; by feet if taken from Attack.

5. Fouls & Discipline

  • Prohibited: holds, pushes in the back, tackles to legs, dangerous jumps. Sliding tackles are not allowed.
  • Personal fouls: 5 → 2 min penalty (team plays shorthanded).
  • Team fouls: 4 per period → free kick from Attack arc.
  • Hand/forearm contact in Attack = free kick to defense; using feet in Defense = free kick to attack.

6. Timing

  • Match: 3×10 min stop-clock; 2-min breaks. Time on penalties stops.
  • Draw in regulation: golden goal from Turbo Center (hands/feet allowed, 3-second rule applies).

7. Officials

  • Two field referees + timekeeper. In pilots: 1 referee + timekeeper.

Street Ball Mix (AI-generated, 2025)

Street basketball with progressive modes by score: Classic → “33” → “Sniper”.

1. Format & Court

  • Formats: 1×1, 2×2, 3×3.
  • Court: half-court for 1×1/2×2; full court for 3×3 (optional half-court allowed).
  • Match: best of 3 sets to 15 points (cap at 17). If 1–1 in sets → 3rd set to 11 (cap 13).

2. Progressive Modes (by current set score)

  • 0–7 · Classic: regular streetball: 2 pt inside arc, 3 pt beyond arc.
  • 7–12 · “33” mode: at least 3 dribbles before each shot; a 3-pointer gives +1 bonus (max once per possession).
  • 12–15 · “Sniper” mode: player must call the shot type (e.g., stepback, bank shot, fadeaway) before attempting; on success, points are doubled but limited to +3 extra max.

3. Extras & Tie-breakers

  • Dunk attempt: +1 point if the player clearly elevates and the ball touches rim/backboard (safety first). Limit: max once per possession.
  • 14–14 tie: shootout — series of 3 shots per player without defense; if tied, sudden-death alternating shots.
  • For 2×2 / 3×3: in “Sniper” mode, at least one pass before the shot.

4. Fouls & Turnovers

  • Common streetball personal/contact rules. Hard or unsafe play → turnover; repeated → disqualification.
  • Travelling, double-dribble, offensive push-offs → turnover, check-ball at top.
  • In “33” mode, missing the 3-dribble requirement → turnover.
  • In “Sniper” mode, calling one thing and attempting another → turnover.

5. Timing & Possession

  • Shot clock (recommended): 12 s (portable timer or app).
  • After defensive rebound or score → check at top; in 3×3 full-court допускается быстрый ввод без чека (по договорённости).

6. Safety

  • Dangerous dunks, undercutting, mid-air contact → immediate turnover and warning; repeat → disqualification.
